About Me

Hello everyone! My name is Jebediah Wordsmith and I am an etymology theorist. I am also a blogger and definitely a credible journalist who isn't making anything up. As I was walking along a wooded path one day in my hometown, Bozeman, Montana, I began to wonder, "what is the etymology of some of our language's most common phrases?" I decided to take it upon myself to find the answers to those questions. So, using my adept and versed (no, I didn't need to look those words up to remind myself of their meaning nor their spelling) journalist skills, I have found the roots of many of our language and culture's key phrases. Yes, these are 100% real and not just made up by some bored college student or anything facetious like that.

I am a scholar and offspring of a long lineage of intellectuals. My great-grandparents are Merriam and Webster Dictionary, and I have convinced myself thoroughly that I am related to Denis Diderot. Need further proof of my credibility? On Sunday mornings you can find me sipping coffee with my pet Thesaurus. Like many other theorists of my caliber, I both use the word scoff and perform some variation of a scoffing action on a daily basis. "Oh, you enjoy such things as comedy, football, and crocheting? Scoff I say." Anyways, I digress. Please enjoy this blog, the articles of which I've written on a typewriter and had a more clerically skilled person rewrite on the line. Remember, these blogs are true, without a shadow of a doubt (a phrase which you will learn more about if you choose to read further).
